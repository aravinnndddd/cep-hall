# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to creating a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful community for all contributors and participants in the CEP Hall project, regardless of their background, identity, or experience level.

We pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, and we are committed to upholding these values.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** - Using welcoming and inclusive language, respecting differing opinions
- **Being constructive** - Providing thoughtful feedback and being open to receiving it
- **Being collaborative** - Working together toward common goals
- **Being patient** - Taking time to understand others and explain your perspective clearly
- **Assuming good intent** - Interpreting actions positively when possible
- **Empowering others** - Helping fellow contributors succeed
- **Welcoming newcomers** - Making new contributors feel valued and supported

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** - Offensive comments, slurs, personal attacks
- **Discrimination** - Based on protected characteristics (race, gender, sexuality, ability, etc.)
- **Intimidation** - Threats, aggressive behavior, or displays of hostility
- **Unwanted attention** - Sexual or otherwise inappropriate comments or advances
- **Disruption** - Deliberately derailing conversations or making discussions unproductive
- **Trolling** - Intentionally inflammatory or provocative comments
- **Doxxing** - Sharing personal information without consent
- **Private disputes in public** - Airing personal conflicts in project spaces

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All GitHub project spaces (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Project-related communications (email, chat)
- Official project events
- Representation of the project in public

This Code of Conduct does **not** cover conduct in private spaces not affiliated with the project.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it by:

**For issues**: Comment politely in the relevant GitHub issue/PR

All reports will be:

- Taken seriously and confidentially
- Investigated thoroughly
- Handled with sensitivity

### Response

Violations may result in:

1. **First offense**: Private conversation/warning with explanation of impact
2. **Pattern of behavior**: Temporary restriction from participation
3. **Serious violations**: Permanent removal from project

The goal is always restoration and education, not punishment.
